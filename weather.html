Temperature, rainfall, and sunlight are among the most important weather factors that impact agriculture. Different crops have different temperature requirements for optimal growth and yield, and too much or too little rainfall can also have significant effects on crop production. For example, prolonged drought can lead to crop failure and reduced yields, while excessive rainfall can cause flooding and waterlogging, which can damage crops and make it difficult for farmers to access their fields.

\

Extreme weather events, such as heatwaves, cold snaps, and severe storms, can also have a significant impact on agriculture. These events can damage crops, livestock, and infrastructure, and can cause disruptions to transportation and supply chains. Understanding the weather and its potential effects on agriculture is critical for farmers and policymakers to make informed decisions about land use, crop selection, and food security.
